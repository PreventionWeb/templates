Top Trends in Disaster Risk

Trends of disaster risk help us to determine whether our DRR efforts are effective

We can sometimes project these trends in order to anticipate future disaster risk patterns and thereby adjust our policies and practices in order to reduce and prepare for these future risks. Disaster risk trends are development indicators. It is therefore essential to identify and understand these patterns.

How to use trends

In order to use trends, we need to be aware of what they represent. Typically they display the results of the analysis of disaster risk data, often direct mortality and economic losses. The trend will depend on many factors, including amongst others:

  • the time period over which it is measured
  • the amount of data included and whether it is based on historical or modelled data
  • improvements in disaster risk reporting
  • the geographical scale (sometimes termed 'resolution') of the data
  • whether they display absolute or relative (comparative) data and the context of the trend
  • what types of disasters or risks the trend represents
  • the statistical significance of the trend

Being aware of these factors helps us to determine whether changes in a trend are due to real changes in disaster risk.

TREND

Disaster risk is still on the rise

UNISDR (2015b) [GAR15]

Twenty-five years after UN Member States adopted the International Decade for Natural Disaster Reduction (IDNDR) and ten years after the adoption of the Hyogo Framework for Action (HFA), disaster risk has not been reduced globally.
